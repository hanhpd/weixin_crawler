Glossary

Main / sub article：The public account sends a single article in 1 + 7 mode. That is, 1 main article and up to 7 side articles. Usually the main article is the protagonist of this push, and the operator has the greatest expectation on the main article.

Depth index：The amount of likes divided by the reading of an article is the depth index of the article. In order to avoid the depth index being a small decimal, the result is multiplied by 1000 and the two digits after the decimal point are retained. Interpretation: The depth index is intuitively the ratio of likes to reading, and then magnified by 1000 times. A higher index indicates that a larger proportion of readers think the article has "depth".

Drop index：The reading of the previous article divided by the reading of this article is multiplying the quotient by 1000 and retaining two decimal places. The smaller the index, the greater the increase in reading this time. The reason behind this is worth exploring. A large drop index indicates a reverse fact.

Review volume：The number of comments is not the number of featured comments at the end of the article, but the total number of all comments, which is generally greater than the number of selected comments. Early public account may not have the message function enabled, so many early public account comments are 0.

Reading data：Reading data is a collective term for readers to add information to an article, including reading, likes, praises, and comments.

median：After a series of numbers is sorted by size, the number in the middle position or the average of the two numbers in the middle is the median.
